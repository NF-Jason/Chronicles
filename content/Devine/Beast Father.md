## The Creation of the First Demigod Animals
At the dawn of time, when the gods still walked among the realms and the world disk spun freshly upon the roots of the World Tree, there existed a sacred realm untouched by mortal hands: the [[Garden of Creation]]. Perched atop the highest branches of, the [[World Tree|Great Spirit of Negentropy]], this celestial garden thrived beyond the reach of time, where reality itself bloomed like flowers upon vines woven from pure essence. It was within this divine sanctuary that the [[Goddess of Life]] and the [[Beast Father]] came together to shape the progenitors of all living creatures—the First Demigod Animals, beings woven from both mortal substance and divine spark. Each was to embody the raw, untamed forces of nature, crafted not as simple beasts, but as eternal archetypes from which all life would flow. 

### The Divine Crafting
These two gods did not create with mere hands, but with thought, will, and sacred rituals older than existence itself. They sang into being, their voices resonating with the cosmic threads that bind all realities. 

From the heart of the garden, where the Well of Origins shimmered with liquid starlight, the gods drew the raw essence of potential. They molded it with care and conflict alike, infusing their creations with fragments of their own power, tempered by the chaotic wildness of nature. 

The [[Beast Father]], whispered into the clay of the land, shaping the fierce hunters and the tireless prey, his breath giving rise to flesh and fur. 

The [[God of the oceans]] the Protector of the Deep, stirred the waters of the Well, birthing ever hunting behemoths and gentle titans to roam the boundless seas. 

The [[Goddess of Storms]], her fingers weaving lightning into feathers, gave wings to the skies, crafting beings that soared with the fury and grace of tempests. 

The [[God of the Mountains]], the Mountain Father, pressed stone and earth together, forging the heartbeats of creatures destined to dwell beneath the world disk, strong and enduring as the mountains themselves. 

### The Setting Upon the World Disk
Once the First Demigod Animals were formed, the gods gathered at the Edge of the Garden, a place where the branches of the World Tree reached out to cradle the world disk below. Here, the gods cast their creations upon the fresh, untamed lands, into the seas yet untouched, and into the skies vast and empty.
The descent was not gentle. As these divine creatures touched the world, their impact shaped the very landscape:
- **Vorgûn**, the Maw-Tyrant, struck the earth with such ferocity that mountains rose and valleys tore open in its wake.
- **[[Auroth]]**, the Ever-Herd, galloped across barren plains, its footsteps sowing seeds of grass and life wherever it roamed.
- **Glaudrith**, the Abyss Fang, plunged into the deepest trenches, carving abyssal scars in the ocean floor with its hunger.
- **Thalmanos**, the Tidebarrer, drifted through the seas, its breath birthing currents and nourishing the first blooms of coral and kelp.
- **Vorthen**, the Sky Maw, tore through the heavens, its wings stirring the first storms and scattering clouds like feathers.
- **Serapha**, the Feathered Flow, painted the skies with light, its soaring form inspiring the dawn and dusk.
- **Shuthor**, the Hollow Maw, burrowed beneath, leaving labyrinthine tunnels that would become the veins of the underworld.
- **Urthil**, the Burrowed Heart, carved pathways of life through stone, spreading fertile earth even in the deepest dark.

The Legacy of the First Demigod Animals These demigod beasts were not merely creatures; they were the pregenitors of all life, each a living template from which countless species would diverge. Their offspring bore diluted echoes of divine power, spreading across the world disk, adapting, evolving, and flourishing. Yet the First Demigod Animals themselves did not vanish. Some retreated into hidden places, deep forests, vast oceans, storm-swept peaks, and the hollow darkness beneath the earth, their forms now legends, their existence woven into myth and worship. Their breath became the wind. Their blood, the rivers. Their bones, the mountains. And their spirits? They linger still, watching the world they helped shape, bound to the eternal cycle of life and death upon the world disk.
